Why Your Natural Health Brand Needs a Blog (Even If You Hate Writing)

By Marina · 7 min read

You started your brand because you are passionate about herbal medicine, natural skincare, or helping people live healthier lives. You did not start it because you love writing 800-word articles about SEO.

And that is completely fine. But here is the thing: if your brand does not have a blog, you are invisible to the people who are actively searching for what you sell.

Let me explain why that matters, and why it is simpler than you think.

Your customers are Googling their problems right now

Think about how you search for things. When your knee hurts, you Google “natural remedies for knee pain.” When you cannot sleep, you Google “herbal teas for insomnia.” When your skin breaks out, you Google “natural skincare for sensitive skin.”

Your customers do the same thing. Every single day, thousands of Australians are typing questions into Google that relate directly to your products. And if you do not have a blog answering those questions, someone else is answering them instead.

That someone else is not recommending your products.

• • •

Instagram is rented land

I know what you are thinking. “I post on Instagram. Isn’t that enough?”

Instagram is fantastic for building community and connecting with people who already know you. But it has two major limitations.

First, your posts disappear. An Instagram post has a lifespan of about 24–48 hours. After that, almost nobody sees it again. A blog post, on the other hand, can bring new visitors to your website for years.

Second, you do not own it. Instagram can change its algorithm tomorrow and your reach drops by half. It has happened before. Your blog lives on your website, which you control completely.

Think of Instagram as the place where people who already love you come to connect. Your blog is how new people who have never heard of you find you for the first time.

• • •

You do not need to write every day

The biggest misconception about blogging is that it requires constant output. It does not. Two to four well-written, strategically targeted blog posts per month is enough to build real search traffic over time.

The key is not volume. It is relevance. One blog post that answers a specific question your customers are Googling is worth more than fifty posts about random topics nobody is searching for.

For example, if you sell herbal teas, a post titled “Best herbal teas for anxiety and stress” could bring hundreds of visitors to your site every single month because people search for that phrase constantly. A post titled “Our founder’s favourite morning routine” is nice, but nobody is searching for it.

• • •

What should you actually write about?

Start with the questions your customers already ask you. If people regularly ask you “what’s the difference between a tincture and a tea?” or “which essential oil is best for headaches?” or “is your skincare safe during pregnancy?” then those are your blog topics.

Each question becomes a blog post. Each blog post becomes a doorway into your brand.

Here are five starter ideas that work for almost every natural health or beauty brand:

The “what is” post

e.g. “What is Kunzea oil and why is it good for joint pain?”

The “how to” post

e.g. “How to build a simple natural skincare routine”

The “versus” post

e.g. “Tea tree vs lavender: which should you use?”

The “benefits” post

e.g. “7 benefits of passionflower you probably didn’t know”

The “guide” post

e.g. “A beginner’s guide to aromatherapy”

Each of these post types targets a real search query. They bring real people to your website. And they position your brand as the expert your customers trust.
